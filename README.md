# lung-mechanics-distributed-simulation
Comparing Normal, Asthma and Emphysema Respiratory Responses


## **1. Introduction**

This document describes the modelling and simulation of human lung mechanics using MATLAB Simulink. **Distributed** models are investigated to analyse respiratory behaviour under normal and pathological conditions such as **asthma** and **emphysema**. The purpose of this study is to demonstrate the effect of varying physiological parameters‚Äîsuch as airway resistance and lung/chest wall compliance‚Äîon airflow, pressure, and lung volume.

---

## **2. Lumped vs Distributed Lung Models**

### **2.1 Lumped Model**

A lumped lung model simplifies the entire respiratory system into a single compartment. In this approach:

* The lung is represented using a single **resistance‚Äìcompliance (R‚ÄìC)** structure.
* Airflow and pressure are assumed to be **uniform** throughout the lung.
* The model is mathematically simple and computationally efficient.
* It is suitable for basic respiratory simulation and introductory physiological studies.

### **2.2 Distributed Model**

A distributed model divides the lung into **multiple interconnected compartments**, each with its own resistance and compliance. This approach:

* Captures **regional variations** in airflow and pressure.
* Provides a more **realistic representation** of lung physiology.
* Allows simulation of diseases affecting specific lung regions.
* Requires more computational resources due to increased complexity.

### **2.3 Comparison of Lumped and Distributed Models**

| Feature                      | Lumped Model       | Distributed Model     |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------ | --------------------- |
| Complexity                   | Low                | High                  |
| Realism                      | Moderate           | High                  |
| Physiological accuracy       | Limited            | High                  |
| Disease modelling capability | Basic              | Detailed              |
| Computational effort         | Low                | Moderate to High      |
| Structural representation    | Single compartment | Multiple compartments |

## **3. Analogy Between Model Parameters and Human Physiology**

| Model Parameter                | Physiological Equivalent                       | Interpretation in Human Body                                              |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Rp (Airway Resistance)**     | Bronchial airway diameter, mucus obstruction   | Higher Rp indicates obstructed airflow, characteristic of asthma.         |
| **Cw (Chest Wall Compliance)** | Flexibility of chest wall and thoracic tissues | Lower Cw corresponds to stiff chest walls, seen in emphysema or fibrosis. |
| **Cl (Lung Compliance)**       | Elasticity of alveolar tissue                  | Reduced Cl indicates stiff lungs; increased Cl indicates hyperinflation.  |
| **Flow Input (Q)**             | Breathing effort by respiratory muscles        | Represents inhalation and exhalation rates generated by the diaphragm.    |
| **Pressure Output**            | Alveolar and pleural pressure                  | Reflects lung inflation and recoil behaviour.                             |
| **Volume Integrator**          | Tidal volume accumulation                      | Tracks the amount of air entering the lungs per breath.                   |

## **9. Conclusion**

The study demonstrates that distributed lung models provide a more detailed and physiologically accurate representation of respiratory mechanics compared to lumped models. Variations in airway resistance and compliance significantly affect airflow, pressure, and tidal volume. Disease-specific simulations (asthma and emphysema) align with known clinical behaviour‚Äîsupporting the validity of the model.

This analysis highlights how lung mechanics change under pathological conditions and reinforces the importance of resistance and compliance in determining respiratory function.
